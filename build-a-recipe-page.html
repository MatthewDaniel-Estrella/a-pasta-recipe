Capellini D'Angelo in Artichoke & Garlic Sauce with Sautéed Broccoli toppings from 10,000 Pasta by Susanna Tee

Cappelini D'Angelo otherwise known as angel hair, these long, thin strands of pasta are often coiled into what look like birds' nests. When cooked, these seperate into single strands. As this is a very delicate pasta, it goes especially well with light sauces.

Ingredients

    Pasta

  • 400 g or 14 oz dried capellini d'angelo
  • salted rapidly boiling water in a large saucepan
  • large knob of butter or drizzle of olive oil
  • pepper to taste

    Sauce

  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 60 g or 2¹/₄ oz butter
  • 3 shallots, finely chopped
  • 6 large garlic cloves, chopped
  • 2 x (400 g or 14 oz) cans artichoke hearts, drained and halved
  • 3 tbsp chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley large garlic cloves, chopped
  • salt and pepper to taste

    Toppings

  • 225 g or 8 oz broccoli
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 tbsp freshly grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions

    Pasta

  1. Cook 400 g/14 oz dried capellini d'angelo in a large saucepan of salted rapidly boiling water for 2-3 minutes or following packet instructions, until just tender. Be careful not to overcook.
  2. Drain, reserving 2 tablespoons of the water in the pan.
  3. Return the pasta to the pan, add a large knob of butter or drizzle of olive oil, season with pepper and toss together.
  4. Serve with warmed bowls with the artichoke and garlic sauce with sautéed broccoli toppings.

    Sauce

  1. Heat the oil and butter in a frying pan.
  2. Add the shallots and fry for 2-3 minutes until softened and beginning to brown.
  3. Add the garlic and cook for 30 seconds.
  4. Add the artichoke hearts and parsley and heat for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Toss with cooked capellini d'angelo.

    Toppings

  1. Cut the broccoli florets from the thick stalk and cut the larger pieces into small florets.
  2. Heat the oil in a frying pan.
  3. Add the broccoli florets and stir-fry over a medium-high heat for 5 minutes until softened and beginning to brown.
  4. Turn into a bowl, add the Parmesan and toss together.
  5. Sprinkle on top of your cooked capellini d'angelo.
